# Non Player Characters

In epic fantasy, and Treacherous Winds, anything from people, institutions,
cultures, to magic itself can be a character.

Make an Antagonist Sheet to track the bonds, reputations, drives, questions,
and any notes related to the PCs Antagonists. Fill it in as Antagonists are
established. Only the player can answer their arc questions and therefore who
their antagonist is, but you can introduce NPCs of the appropriate type
(Monsters, Zealots, Puppetmasters, Tyrants, ...) to give them ample opportunity
to answer their Arc questions.

As a GM you'll do world building through creating non player characters (NPCs)
on NPC sheets and NPC cards. Sheets are for the big deep anchors of the world.
Make NPC Sheets for all Terrain, Institutions, Movements, and Monsters that
anchor the story.

You can make and update these sheets as prep before or between games, and you
can make and update these sheets as they become established during play. You
can do it yourself as the GM or you can do it collaboratively with the other
players. Let your personal style dictate that.

As you play note anything relevant that you'd like to keep track of, especially
bonds and reputations. Note questions you're curious to answer through the
course of play

We learn about any of these characters much in the same way that we learn about
characters in epic fantasy literature. They may be introduced in a flash or
slowly. They may be foreshadowed, disclosing details as hints and clues. A
massive claw mark on a cupboard, droppings, a whisper passing through a crowd
etc.

## NPC Sheets and Cards

NPCs can be combinations of multiple archetypes. For instance a Mercenary
Company could be an Army/Guard + Merchant/Craft Guild. An antoganist could be a
Dragon + Zealot + Puppetmaster.

For minor characters, create an **NPC Card**. Write their name, type(s),
drive(s), and a question you're curious about. Make quick notes about their
appearance, other NPCs and how they are related, any bonds or reputations.
Leave room for more. When you run out of space, decide to either erace some
notes that have become irrelevant or upgrade them to an NPC Sheet.

For major characters, create an **NPC Sheet** by following the procedures
listed in the NPC types sections below.

## Antagonist Sheet (Maybe this is a connections sheet)

If this is a connections sheet, write character names with lines between them
naming bonds, reputations, hopes, plans, etc.


### Monster

**Drive:** To erode or destroy humanity.
**Questions:**
- How is the presence of the monster felt?
- What are the early signs that a monster is around?
- Who can ignore the signs? Who cannot?
- What does the monster gain by this?

#### Dragon

**Drive:** To consume and hoard.
**Questions:**
- What is a dragon? Does it breathe fire? Is it intelligent? Magical?
- What are its strengths? Weaknesses?
- What does it consume? What does it hoard?
- Does it communicate or coordinate with people? If so, how?

### Institution

**Drive:** To maintain order. To consolidate power.
**Questions:**
- Who offers and who holds power? 
- How are they chosen, and how do they keep or lose it?
- What is the structure or hierarchy?
- What order does the institution create and maintain?
- What secrets does it have to keep to maintain order and power?
- How is credit and blame determined?

#### Kingdom/Nation

**Drive:** To prosper.
**Questions:**
- What unifies the people in the kingdom?
- What devides the people in the kingdom? Is the devision by law?
- Who struggles with prosperity in this kingdom and who doesn't?
- Who can decide to stay or leave? Why do they make the choice they do?

#### Religion/Faith/Church

**Drive:** To convince and console.
**Questions:**
- What morality does it promote and what morality does it follow?
- What is its mythology and how are its stories told?
- Where do the faithful congregate?
- What are their ceremonies, protocols, and rituals?
